Pierogi

While I didn't grow up eating Pierogi, it definitely feels like a homey comfort meal for me. It's a great snack for cold times! It also has a lot of potential to be made vegan friendly. There are plenty of different fillings that people use but potato, onion and cottage cheese is a classic.

Ingredients

Wrapper

  • 250g flour
  • 40g butter
  • 1/2 cup water
  • 1 tsp salt
  • 1 egg

Filling

  • 1kg potato
  • 2 large onions
  • 200g cottage cheese
  • Salt, pepper and sugar to taste

Toppings

  • Sour cream
  • Herbs (parsley or dill)

Method

Wrapper

  1. Mix flour and salt in bowl.
  2. Melt butter and water in saucepand then slowly incorporate into bowl.
  3. Finally, add whisked egg and knead for 5-7 mins before covering with wrap

Filling

  1. Peel potatoes and cut into smaller pieces
  2. While potatos cook, dice onion and cook on low with generous amount of butter, salt, pepper and a little sugar
  3. Once translucent and golden, set aside a small bowl for garnish and add rest to filling bowl
  4. Once potatoes are done add to filling bowl along with cottage cheese, mash then cool

Cooking

  1. Break off small bits (maybe 1/4) of dough to work at a time
  2. Roll really thin (think 3mm or so) and cut into rounds
  3. Put filling in and pinch around the edges to make a seal
  4. Boil until it floats and gets a little fluffy - You can then store them for later

Serving

  1. Put a good amount of butter in the pan
  2. Pan fry the pierogi until crispy
  3. Heat up some garnish onion also
  4. Serve with sour cream and herbs
